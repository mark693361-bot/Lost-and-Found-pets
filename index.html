📋 Animal Reference Guide

🐕 Dog

  • Include the breed (or closest mix), exact coat colour, and any markings — white chest patch, black saddle, brindle stripes, etc.
  • Note ear type (floppy, upright, cropped) and tail shape (curled, docked, long and feathered).
  • Mention collar colour, tag text, or microchip number if known.
  • Dogs often stay within 1–2 km of where they were lost; check parks, alleys, and neighbouring streets first.
  • A scared dog may hide under cars or porches — search at dawn and dusk when it is quieter.

🐈 Cat

  • Describe the coat pattern precisely — tabby, tortoiseshell, calico, solid, or pointed (Siamese-style).
  • Note eye colour; heterochromia (two different colours) is very distinctive.
  • State whether the cat is indoor-only or indoor/outdoor — indoor cats rarely go further than the immediate garden.
  • Cats are most active at dawn and dusk; search under decks, in sheds, and inside drains.
  • Leave unwashed bedding or litter outside — familiar scents draw cats home.

🐦 Bird

  • Describe exact feather colours per region — head, wings, chest, tail, beak — and any mutations (lutino, albino, pied).
  • Note any leg bands — colour and number sequence can identify the breeder and owner.
  • List any words or phrases the bird can say — a bird calling its own name is a strong identifier.
  • Birds travel kilometres quickly; contact avian vets, bird clubs, and pet shops nearby.

🐾 Other Animals

  • Rabbits: note ear length, lop vs. upright, coat colour and length.
  • Small rodents: specify size, colour, and any patches. Search at night.
  • Reptiles: include species name, length, and scale colour pattern.
  • Exotic pets: note any legal ownership documents shelters may need.
Tip: "Golden retriever mix, cream chest, red collar, answers to Biscuit" gets far more responses than "brown dog, medium size."

🔍 Browse — Reference Guide

How to Search Effectively

  • Scan all listings carefully — look beyond the name and check breed, colour, and size.
  • Check when the pet was posted. Listings older than 2 weeks are flagged with an amber border.
  • Use the Near me filter to see pets lost close to your location.

If You Think You Found a Match

  • Tap Reveal contact on the listing and reach out immediately — time is critical.
  • Offer to send a photo to confirm it's the right animal before arranging a meetup.
  • Arrange the reunion in a familiar, safe location for the pet.

Marking a Pet as Found

  • Use the I Found This Pet! button once you've confirmed the match.
  • The listing is removed from the board to keep results up to date.
Tip: Use the QR code button to generate a printable tag for a pet's collar — anyone who scans it can contact the owner instantly.

📍 Report Found Pet — Guide

What to Do Immediately

  • Ensure the animal is safe. Keep it away from traffic and other animals.
  • Check for a collar tag or microchip — vets can scan chips for free.
  • Take a clear photo in good lighting so owners can confirm the match.

Writing a Good Description

  • Include specific colours, markings, and distinctive features.
  • Note the approximate size and estimated breed if known.
  • Describe the animal's behaviour — calm, scared, friendly.
  • Be precise about the location: street name, nearby landmark, or neighbourhood.

Next Steps After Reporting

  • Browse the missing pets list — the app will auto-match by animal type.
  • If the animal appears injured, contact your local vet or animal shelter.
Tip: If you can temporarily foster the animal, mention it in your description — it greatly improves the pet's chances of a safe reunion.
