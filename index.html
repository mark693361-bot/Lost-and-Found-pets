📋 Report Lost Pet — Animal Reference Guide

🐕 Dog

  • Include the breed (or closest mix), exact coat colour, and any markings — white chest patch, black saddle, brindle stripes, etc.
  • Note ear type (floppy, upright, cropped) and tail shape (curled, docked, long and feathered).
  • Mention collar colour, tag text, or microchip number if known.
  • Dogs often stay within 1–2 km of where they were lost; check parks, alleys, and neighbouring streets first.
  • A scared dog may hide under cars or porches — search at dawn and dusk when it is quieter.

🐈 Cat

  • Describe the coat pattern precisely — tabby (mackerel, classic, spotted), tortoiseshell, calico, solid, pointed (Siamese-style).
  • Note eye colour; cats often have two different-coloured eyes (heterochromia), which is very distinctive.
  • State whether the cat is indoor-only or indoor/outdoor — indoor cats rarely go further than the immediate garden.
  • Cats are most active at dawn and dusk; search under decks, in sheds, and inside drains.
  • Leave an unwashed piece of bedding or litter outside — familiar scents draw cats home.

🐦 Bird

  • Describe exact feather colours per body region — head, wings, chest, tail, beak — and any colour mutations (lutino, albino, pied).
  • Note any leg bands (colour and number sequence) — these can identify the bird's breeder and owner.
  • If the bird can talk or mimic sounds, list any known words or phrases — a bird calling its own name is a strong identifier.
  • Birds can travel several kilometres quickly; contact local avian vets, bird clubs, and pet shops in your area.
  • Play a recording of the species' call outdoors — it may attract the bird back.

🐾 Other Animals

  • Rabbits: note ear length, lop vs. upright, coat colour and length. Domestic rabbits rarely travel far but can hide in dense vegetation.
  • Small rodents (hamster, guinea pig): specify size, colour, and any distinguishing patches. Search at night — they are most active after dark.
  • Reptiles: include species name, length, and scale colour pattern. Contact local reptile societies and reptile-specialist vets.
  • Exotic/uncommon pets: attach a photo description and note any legal ownership documents — shelters may need these before returning the animal.
General tip: The more specific your listing, the faster a match is confirmed. Vague descriptions like "brown dog, medium size" get far fewer responses than "golden retriever mix, cream chest, red collar, answers to Biscuit."

🔍 Browse Missing Pets — Reference Guide

How to Search Effectively

  • Scan all listings carefully — descriptions may be incomplete; look beyond the name and check breed, colour, and size.
  • Check when the pet was posted. Recent posts are more likely still missing.
  • Note the area. A pet rarely travels far from where it was last seen.

If You Think You Found a Match

  • Contact the owner as soon as possible — time is critical.
  • Offer to send a photo to confirm it's the right animal before arranging a meetup.
  • Arrange the reunion in a familiar, safe location for the pet.

Marking a Pet as Found

  • Use the I Found This Pet! button on any listing once you've confirmed the match.
  • The owner is notified automatically via the Notifications tab.
  • The listing is removed from the board to keep results up to date.
Tip: Even if no listing matches, use Report Found Pet to log the animal you found — the owner may post a missing report later and see your report in Notifications.

📍 Report Found Pet — Reference Guide

What to Do Immediately

  • Ensure the animal is safe. Keep it away from traffic and other animals.
  • Check for a collar tag or microchip number — vets can scan for chips for free.
  • Take a clear photo in good lighting so owners can confirm the match.

Writing a Good Description

  • Include specific colours, markings, and any distinctive features (scars, patches, eye colour).
  • Note the approximate size and estimated breed if known.
  • Describe the animal's behaviour — calm, scared, friendly — this helps owners recognise their pet.
  • Be precise about the location: street name, nearby landmark, or neighbourhood.

Next Steps After Reporting

  • Browse the missing pets list to see if there's already a matching post.
  • If the animal appears injured or distressed, contact your local vet or animal shelter.
  • Share on local community boards or social media to reach more potential owners.
Tip: If you can temporarily foster the animal, mention it in your description — it greatly improves the pet's chances of a safe reunion.
